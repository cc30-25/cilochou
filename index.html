Basketball

I am a huge sports girl, and growing up, I wasn't a fan of failure. Under the strike of game loss, I would deny every little step our team had made no matter how cheerful it was.
In my senior year of high school, I deadly wanted to win something for the team. But before the most important final game, I got injured. Sitting there watch the whole team struggle, but couldn't do anything with it, truly hit me differently. It made me appreciate never than ever how precious it was to simply be able to be in this team and enjoy basketball.
We lost, but I didn't feel the frustration as I used to. I understood something fundamental about myself: Achievement isn't just about the final result but more about the persistence and determination that drives each step forward. This journey not only taught me to master the unexpectations in sports but strengthened my drive to carve my own path.
